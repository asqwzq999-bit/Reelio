Why download without a watermark?

Videos saved directly from the TikTok app get a username and logo stamped into a corner of the frame. That's harmless for personal viewing, but it becomes a problem when you:

  • Reuse part of a clip in an edit, lecture slide, or presentation
  • Stitch multiple clips together
  • Add new subtitles to a video

In these cases, a clean source makes the final result look far more polished. Reelio focuses on providing exactly that: the original quality stream, without the watermark.

How to save on each device

💻 Saving on PC

On desktop or laptop browsers, just follow these steps.

  1. Open the TikTok website in your browser.
  2. Click the share icon on the video and choose "Copy link."
  3. Paste the link into the box on this page and hit download.
  4. Click the format (MP4/MP3) button you want on the result screen.

📱 Saving on iPhone

Works directly in Safari — no extra app needed.

  1. Open the video in the TikTok app, tap Share → Copy link.
  2. Switch to Safari, open this page, and paste the link.
  3. Tap download, then tap the MP4 button.
  4. The file shows up in the Files app's Downloads folder.

🤖 Saving on Android

Works the same way in Chrome or any default Android browser.

  1. In the TikTok app, tap Share → Copy link.
  2. Open this page in your browser and paste the link.
  3. Tap download, then tap the MP4 button.
  4. The file saves automatically to your device's Downloads folder.

MP4 or MP3 — which should you pick?

If you plan to keep the video or use it in further editing, go with MP4. If all you need is the background music or a voice track, MP3 is the better fit.

  • MP4 — full original file with picture and sound, ready to cut and re-assemble
  • MP3 — much smaller, ready to use for listening or as editing audio

Not sure which one you need? Grab the MP4 first and extract audio later.
